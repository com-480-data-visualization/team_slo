The Race for Medals: A Century of History

The Olympic Games are the scene of incredible performances and emotional moments that are forever etched in the minds of athletes and fans alike. But when it comes to results, and particularly medal counts, the countries seem very unequal. In both winter and summer, the USA outdid its rivals. On this site, you can discover the reasons behind this domination through various graphs.

Sporting Spectrum: A Nation's Athletic Showcase

A first case study is the presence of a country in the disciplines featured at the Olympic Games. Clearly, some countries have very uneven situations depending on the season. Countries in the southern hemisphere will find it harder to field athletes at the winter games, while Nordic countries will have the opposite tendency. On the other hand, countries with temperate climates are more likely to achieve good results in both seasons. Use this visualization to discover the distribution for the country of your choice!
